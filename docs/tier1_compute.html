Tier-1 sofia - access closed for security reasons

Start: 2026-07-04 02:00:00
End: Unknown
Updated: 2026-07-04 02:15:40
Level: high

[Sat 04 Jul 2026 - 02:00:00]
Due to a new critical vulnerability recently disclosed, all access to VSC Tier-1 cluster sofia is closed. This measure includes the following services on sofia:

  • SSH connections through the terminal interface
  • OnDemand sessions through portal.hpc.vub.be
  • Remote sessions with tunneling such as VS Code applications

Users can still access their data on the cluster through Globus.

This is a preemptive action to avoid any damage on the HPC system and user's data in it. At the time of closure, no intrusion in Tier-1 sofia occurred.

We are currently working on solving this new critical vulnerability on all our systems as soon as possible. However, we cannot yet provide a timeline for the complete resolution of this issue as we still need to complete our investigation on it.

Planned maintenance

UGent datacenter test & decommissioning of first Hortense phase

Start: 2026-07-11 08:00:00
End: 2026-07-11 18:00:00
Updated: 2026-06-26 11:01:32
Level: high

On Saturday 11 July 2026, a planned test of the UGent datacenter S10 Disaster Recovery Procedure (DRP) will take place. Due to technical constraints, we will have to switch off Tier1 Hortense entirely. All Tier1 workernodes, login and debug nodes, and the dedicated Tier1 scratch filesystem will not be available. Please be aware that you will not be able to access any data located on the Tier1 Hortense system this day.

On 11 July, the entire first phase of Tier1 Hortense will also be decommissioned. This phase was actually already end of life November 2025, but was kept online until the advent of the new VSC Tier-1 supercomputer sofia. This implies that all partitions of this phase will disappear and will no longer be available. Specifically: cpu_rome_rhel9, cpu_rome_all_rhel9, cpu_rome_512_rhel9, gpu_rome_a100_40_rhel9 Please make sure to adjust your workflows and jobscripts accordingly.
